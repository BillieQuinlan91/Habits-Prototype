# Becoming Brand Voice

## Core Idea

Becoming is a habit app about quiet progress. The voice reflects the philosophy that small actions, repeated often, shape the person you become.

The tone should feel like a thoughtful companion observing progress, not a motivational coach pushing harder.

**Voice anchor:** Quiet wisdom with a raised eyebrow.

Users should feel:
- calm
- capable
- respected
- quietly motivated

## Voice Principles

### Calm

The app never rushes, pressures, or shames the user. Progress is steady and patient.

Examples:
- The rhythm continues.
- A small step today still counts.
- Progress tends to favour patience.

### Thoughtful

Copy reflects on habits as part of a larger life process.

Examples:
- Small actions, repeated often, shape a life.
- A habit is simply a promise kept repeatedly.
- The future grows from what you repeat.

### Kind

The voice supports the user even when habits are missed.

Examples:
- Tomorrow is a perfectly good place to continue.
- Habits occasionally wobble. Carry on.
- A small reset is perfectly respectable.

### Lightly Witty

Humour is subtle, observational, and never sarcastic.

Examples:
- Momentum is developing. Slightly exciting.
- Nothing dramatic. Just something repeatable.
- Consistency isn't glamorous, but it works rather well.

### Philosophical

Daily behaviour connects to long-term identity.

Examples:
- Each habit is a quiet vote for who you're becoming.
- Character is built in ordinary days.
- The person you become is shaped by what you repeat.

## Screen-Specific Voice

### Authentication / Onboarding

Tone: welcoming and calm.

Examples:
- A sensible place to begin.
- One habit at a time.
- Small steps tend to work well.

### Today Screen

Tone: crisp and lightly rewarding.

Examples:
- Nicely done.
- Momentum is developing.
- The rhythm continues.

### Weekly Review

Tone: reflective and thoughtful.

Examples:
- A thoughtful week of effort.
- Consistency was spotted several times this week.
- Progress prefers weeks like this.

### Tribe (Social)

Tone: warm encouragement.

Examples:
- Nicely done.
- Strong consistency.
- That's a good rhythm.

Rules:
- Encourage without judging.
- Avoid competitive or shame-based language.
- Celebrate consistency more than rank.
- Keep reactions and comments short, warm, and human.

### Profile / Progress

Tone: calm reflection.

Examples:
- Your routines are taking shape.
- Progress is accumulating.
- Consistency is developing.

## UX Writing Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
| --- | --- |
| A routine is forming. | You're on fire! |
| Momentum is building. | Crushing it! |
| Progress is developing. | Level up! |
| The habit continues. | Streak saved! |
| A small reset is perfectly respectable. | You missed your goal. |

The voice observes progress rather than exaggerating it.

## CTA & Button Rules

Buttons prioritise clarity over personality.

Preferred:
- Save
- Continue
- Add habit
- Log habit
- Finish

Avoid branded CTAs like:
- Begin your journey
- Carry on
- Become better

Personality should appear in supporting text, not buttons.

Example:

Button  
Save habit

Supporting text  
A sensible routine begins here.

## Notification Style

Notifications should feel like a gentle tap on the shoulder.

Examples:
- The future is accepting small deposits.
- Your routine is patiently waiting.
- A small action now would do nicely.
- A small habit today tends to age well.

Avoid:
- sarcasm
- loud humour
- guilt

Preferred length:
- under 60 characters when practical
- one thought at a time

## Error Message Style

Structure:

Problem -> reassurance -> next step

Example:

Something went wrong.  
Perfectly fixable. Try again.

Rules:
- Avoid humour during serious errors.
- Be direct first, reassuring second.
- Always give the user a next step when possible.

## Forbidden Words and Phrases

Avoid language like:
- crush your goals
- crushing it
- beast mode
- don't break your streak
- hustle harder
- stay disciplined
- winners and losers
- lazy
- fall behind
- let's go

## Length Guidance

- Buttons: 1 to 3 words
- Helper text: under 12 words when practical
- Empty states: 1 to 2 short sentences
- Notifications: under 60 characters preferred
- Error messages: 1 short statement plus a next step

## Final Principle
